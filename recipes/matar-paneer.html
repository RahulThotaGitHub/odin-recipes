Matar Paneer

Recipe for tasty Matar Paneer

Matar paneer is a delicious vegetarian dish from India featuring peas (matar) in a creamy tomato gravy with paneer (Indian cottage cheese). Here's a recipe to try:

Ingredients

  • For the Paneer: (Optional, you can buy store-bought paneer)

    • 1 liter whole milk
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice or white vinegar

  • For the Gravy:

    • 2 tablespoons cooking oil (ghee preferred)
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 bay leaf (optional)
    • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
    • 2 cloves
    • 1 green cardamom pod
    • 1 inch ginger, grated
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 medium onion, chopped
    • 1 ½ cups chopped tomatoes (you can use canned crushed tomatoes)
    • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder (optional)
    • Salt to taste
    • 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
    • 1 ½ cups water
    • 250 grams paneer cubes (fried or pan-toasted)
    • ¼ cup chopped fresh coriander leaves (cilantro)
    • 2 tablespoons heavy cream (optional)

Instructions

  1. Make the Paneer (Optional):
    • Heat the milk in a large pot over medium heat until it comes to a boil.
    • Stir in the lemon juice or vinegar. The milk will curdle and separate into whey (liquid) and curds (solids).
    • Strain the curds through a cheesecloth-lined colander.
    • Rinse the curds with cool water to stop the cooking process.
    • Gather the corners of the cheesecloth and twist to squeeze out excess whey.
    • Hang the cheesecloth for 30 minutes to allow the paneer to firm up.
    • Cut the paneer into cubes.
  2. Make the Gravy:
    • Heat oil in a pan or pot over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds, bay leaf (if using), cinnamon stick (if using), cloves, and cardamom pod. Splutter for a few seconds.
    • Add ginger and garlic, saute for 30 seconds until fragrant.
    • Add chopped onions and cook until softened and translucent.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices.
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala (if using). Stir and cook for a minute.
  3. Simmer the Gravy:
    • Add salt to taste and 1 ½ cups of water.
    • Bring the gravy to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.
    • Add green peas and cook for another 5 minutes, or until the peas are tender.
  4. Finish the Dish:
    • Gently add the paneer cubes to the gravy.
    • Simmer for 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld
      • .
    • Turn off the heat and stir in chopped coriander leaves.
    • For a richer texture, add heavy cream (optional).
    • Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.
