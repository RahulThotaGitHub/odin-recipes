Kheer

Recipe for tasty kheer

Kheer is a classic Indian rice pudding known for its creamy texture and rich flavor. Here's a basic recipe to get you started:

Ingredients

  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • ¼ to ½ cup basmati rice (or any other starchy rice)
  • 4 to 6 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • ¼ to ½ teaspoon cardamom powder (or 2 to 3 cardamom pods)
  • 10 cashews or 10 almonds (sliced or slivered)
  • 10 pistachios (sliced or chopped)
  • A pinch of saffron (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon rose water or kewra water (optional)
  • Raisins (optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly.
  2. In a pot or pressure cooker, combine rice, 1 cup of milk, and 1 cup of water. Cook the rice until soft. You can use 2 cups of milk altogether if using a pot and simmering until soft.
  3. Pour in the remaining milk and bring it to a boil.
  4. Lower the heat and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the milk thickens and reduces by half. The rice should be completely cooked and creamy.
  5. Add sugar, cardamom powder, and nuts. Simmer for a few more minutes.
  6. If using saffron, add a pinch and simmer for a minute to release the flavor.
  7. For a richer taste, add rose water or kewra water (optional).
  8. Adjust the consistency of the kheer by adding more milk for a thinner consistency or simmering longer for a thicker pudding.
  9. Garnish with nuts, raisins (if using), and serve warm or chilled.
